Swami Vivekanand

(1863-1902)

Patriot Saint of Modern India

  • Early Life and Education: Narendranath was a bright student, excelling in music, sports, and studies. He graduated from the Scottish Church College with a degree in philosophy.
  • Spiritual Awakening: His meeting with Ramakrishna at the Dakshineswar Kali Temple was a turning point, leading him to embrace spirituality and renounce worldly pleasures.
  • Parliament of the World's Religions: In 1893, he gained international recognition by delivering an inspiring speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago, where he began his address with "Sisters and Brothers of America," receiving a standing ovation.
  • Teachings: Swami Vivekananda emphasized the importance of self-awareness, meditation, and the unity of all religions. He also promoted the idea of "Practical Vedanta" and encouraged social service.
  • Ramakrishna Mission: He founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, dedicated to social service, education, and spiritual development
  • Literary Contributions: Vivekananda was a prolific writer and speaker. His works include "Raja Yoga," "Karma Yoga," "Bhakti Yoga," and numerous lectures and letters.
  • Legacy: Swami Vivekananda's teachings continue to inspire millions around the world. His birthday, January 12, is celebrated as National Youth Day in India
